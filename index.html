I am a Research Fellow in the Center for Computational Mathematics at the Flatiron Institute. My research focuses on deep generative models and machine learning in the sciences; see here for previous work on PDEs, images, more images, sequential causal effects, heart disease, more heart disease, nonlinear diffusion + active matter, multimodal representations, learning diffusion noise schedules, and understanding generative models' likelihoods. My ongoing work at the flatiron is a mix of theory for diffusion/flows and applications to chemistry and to nuclear fusion energy.


Previously, I completed my PhD at NYU's Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences, advised by Rajesh Ranganath. Before that, I studied music composition.
